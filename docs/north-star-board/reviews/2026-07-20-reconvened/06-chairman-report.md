# Chairman Report

## Overall Board Verdict

The reconvened board is unanimous on the substance:

> Meeting Ingest has proved that it creates valuable meeting records, but it has not yet proved trustworthy continuity across either the next meeting or the accumulated history.

The updated evidence strengthens the product case and raises the release bar. Sustained HTV use is authoritative evidence of recurring value. It is also a mixed-generation corpus whose current validity, dates, identities, duplicate representations, and derivation eligibility are not yet explained by the product. At the same time, frozen and editable executables can run different code while presenting the same `0.1.0` identity.

The product remains an **internal/private alpha**. The board replaces the narrow **Just Works Ingest** framing with **Just Works Continuity**. No canonical roadmap is changed by this report; owner approval remains required.

## Consensus

### Unanimous conclusions

All five seats concluded:

1. The founding conclusion remains directionally valid: the engine is credible, while the low-ceremony product experience is not independently proven.
2. HTV is the authoritative sustained-use corpus. Spelman is additional historical quality evidence. The six copied local records are not independent dogfood.
3. Sustained use proves value more strongly than the founding board knew, but does not prove current-contract compatibility or low ceremony.
4. The real job includes the next meeting **and** accumulated meeting continuity.
5. Build/version/update certainty is a primary product trust feature, not internal release plumbing.
6. Consumer client work needs one approved immutable build and an unmistakable separation from editable development code.
7. Corpus reckoning must move ahead of Stakeholder Briefing and must begin with a deterministic, read-only report.
8. No HTV or Spelman mutation should occur without a separately reviewed and approved adoption plan.
9. Stakeholder Briefing remains unproven until it consumes qualified history with visible coverage and exclusions.
10. Release posture remains internal/private alpha; expansion into more hosts, providers, sources, Guidance, global identity, deeper iQ integration, or public launch must freeze.
11. The ignored six-meeting local runtime state should be retired as one complete set after uniqueness verification and owner approval, not deleted piecemeal.

### Majority emphasis

Four or more seats emphasized:

- a pre-meeting readiness result with exact executable, immutable build, channel, install mode, skill compatibility, privacy/config state, and blocking health;
- a simple user verdict such as `Ready`, `Ready with history warnings`, or `Blocked`;
- exact engine/workflow provenance in runs, artifacts, handoffs, ledgers, and derived outputs;
- one reference host and one normal natural-language workflow;
- honest pending/completion semantics and actionable health severity;
- controlled, dry-run-first recovery instead of manual cache or lock deletion;
- a combined exit gate containing fresh-meeting proof, historical classification, and recovery proof;
- a later non-maintainer acceptance run to reduce developer-user compensation bias.

## Disagreements

The seats disagreed only on implementation posture and wording, not direction.

### Revise or replace the founding milestone

The Jobs and developer seats described Just Works Continuity as a revision of Just Works Ingest. Product management, engineering, and marketing treated the old framing as replaced. The substantive proposal is identical: preserve one-host fresh-ingest proof, but make runtime certainty and history reckoning inseparable from the gate.

Chairman disposition: **replace the milestone name and preserve its original fresh-ingest acceptance intent inside the broader continuity outcome.**

### Whether history warnings block the next ingest

Engineering leaned fail-closed for incompatible runtime and consumer pins. Developer experience proposed a middle state when fresh ingest is safe but history is degraded.

Chairman disposition: **fail closed for runtime, contract, privacy, active-work, or integrity risks; allow `Ready with history warnings` only when the new ingest is safe and the excluded historical scope is explicit.** Such a result cannot satisfy the full Continuity exit gate.

### Editable development builds

Seats agreed editable installs cannot look like approved client builds. Some favored prohibition; others allowed an explicit development override.

Chairman disposition: **block editable builds by default in an approved consumer channel; retain an explicit, visible maintainer override that marks the run and its outputs as development provenance.**

### Naming

Marketing observed that “ingest” describes the entry action rather than the durable benefit, but found no present reason to rename the technical package.

Chairman disposition: **keep `meeting-ingest` during alpha and lead product language with meeting continuity.** Revisit naming only after the continuity outcome is demonstrated.

### Corpus adoption shape

The board could not determine from privacy-safe metadata whether legacy history should be adopted in place, mapped, selectively regenerated, or moved to a clean current root.

Chairman disposition: **no choice yet.** The deterministic read-only report must precede and inform this human decision.

## Personal Chairman Judgment

The new evidence changes my earlier synthesis materially.

The product's soul is not “put in a transcript and get Markdown.” It is:

> A meeting becomes trustworthy project memory, and that memory remains usable when the next meeting arrives.

The product has already found its first real customer: its maintainer. That is not a failure. The failure was treating repeated use as informal background rather than continuously reconciling the power user's accumulated history, failure modes, and installed runtime back into product truth.

The most damaging gap is not that the machinery is complicated; it is that even the person who built the machinery cannot ask it which approved logic will run tomorrow. That violates the trust promise before a transcript enters the inbox. Runtime identity is therefore the first gate inside the milestone.

History cannot be bolted on later. A continuity product that knows only the newest 37 current-schema artifacts while the authoritative corpus contains 190 Markdown objects is not yet coherent. But historical repair must not become an uncontrolled cleanup project. First make the corpus legible, then ask the owner what may change.

I would organize one milestone in this order:

1. **Know what will run.** Establish the approved immutable runtime, workflow compatibility, and pre-meeting readiness verdict.
2. **Know what history means.** Generate a deterministic, privacy-safe, read-only corpus reckoning for HTV and Spelman.
3. **Prove the next meeting.** Complete a fresh real meeting through one reference host using the approved runtime and honest recovery semantics.
4. **Prove continuity.** After separate owner approval, qualify a representative historical slice and use it in a real follow-on task with explicit coverage.

This sequence is not four new roadmaps. It is one product promise proved from release identity through accumulated usefulness.

## Proposed Product Definition

> Meeting Ingest turns each meeting into a trustworthy project record and keeps accumulated meeting history usable and explainable through one approved agent workflow.

Supporting line:

> Trust the next meeting. Keep the history.

The supported alpha can be more concrete:

> Meeting Ingest turns `.txt`, `.vtt`, and `.docx` transcripts into trustworthy meeting records—summary, decisions, actions, evidence, provenance, and source history—and keeps qualified history available to the technical project owner.

## Target User And Job To Be Done

Initial user: the maintainer operating in the highest-frequency selected agent host, followed by similarly technical project owners in private alpha.

Job:

> Before, during, and after a meeting, know that an approved workflow will turn the transcript into a durable record and that prior meeting history remains usable—without inspecting PATH, git state, package receipts, request files, ledgers, or schemas.

## Present Release Posture

### Credible current claims

- The engine supports deterministic `.txt`, `.vtt`, and `.docx` extraction.
- It produces useful summary-plus-verbatim Markdown and structured signals.
- It has credible source identity, provider validation, idempotency, archive, reconcile, and recovery foundations.
- It has 231 passing contract and failure-behavior tests.
- It has sustained power-user use in the authoritative HTV mixed-generation corpus.
- At review time, 37 HTV artifacts displayed current schema and transcript markers.

### Claims that must not be made

- The product knows or enforces which approved build will run in a consumer project.
- The entire HTV corpus is current-contract valid or represents 190 independent current successes.
- The six local copies add six dogfood meetings.
- A normal user can complete and recover the workflow without expert help.
- Stakeholder Briefing is demonstrated against accumulated history.
- The product is self-serve, beta-ready, or broadly supported across named hosts.

## Immediate Trust And Experience Blockers

1. Frozen and editable executables can run different logic under the same version.
2. No immutable build, release channel, consumer pin, update status, or skill compatibility is product-visible.
3. Runs, artifacts, handoffs, ledgers, and derived outputs lack sufficient build provenance.
4. Pending provider work can appear success-class.
5. Health does not distinguish next-meeting blockers from history warnings, recoverable residue, and optional capability.
6. Stale handoff and lock recovery can require manual filesystem judgment.
7. HTV/Spelman history has no deterministic adoption classification or eligibility model.
8. Current-valid counts, physical counts, dates, identities, signals, archive state, and artifacts disagree.
9. The reference host, approved release authority, and consumer pin policy are undecided.
10. Stakeholder Briefing lacks qualified corpus coverage and reviewed identity proof.

## Measurable Release Gates

Just Works Continuity exits only when:

1. One reference host, initial audience, approved release authority, and consumer channel are recorded.
2. One canonical immutable client executable is selected deterministically.
3. A product-visible readiness check reports executable, semantic version, immutable build, channel, install mode, dirty/development state, consumer pin, skill/workflow compatibility, privacy/config state, and update need.
4. Approved client work fails closed on incompatible runtime, skill, privacy, contract, or active integrity state.
5. Editable mode is unmistakable and requires explicit override; development provenance persists in outputs.
6. Run summaries, provider handoffs, artifacts, ledger snapshots, and derived outputs record reconstructable engine/workflow provenance.
7. Pending, partial, completed, and failed states are distinct; pending is never unqualified success.
8. Health groups next-meeting blockers, incomplete active work, corruption, legacy/adoption work, advisories, and optional absence.
9. Stale handoff, stale lock, invalid response, interruption, duplicate redrop, and known commit-window residue have tested, product-owned recovery.
10. A read-only HTV/Spelman scan requires no initialization or writes and deterministically classifies logical bundles as current-valid, legacy-valid, adoptable, repairable, conflicting, ignored, duplicate, or orphaned.
11. Corpus totals reconcile across source, artifact, ledger, signal, processed, archive/done, handoff, date, and identity state with explainable exceptions.
12. Any adoption or repair requires separate approval against a fingerprinted report and preserves legacy provenance and original evidence.
13. One fresh non-synthetic meeting completes from a normal user request in the reference host using the approved build, with no source/PATH/ledger/cache inspection.
14. Completion reports artifact, signal, ledger, archive, reconcile, provider, host, effective date/confidence, and exact build identity.
15. A representative, separately approved historical subset supports a useful continuity task with explicit coverage and exclusions.
16. Stakeholder Briefing remains outside the claim unless the qualified subset, reviewed identity, deterministic rebuild, and usefulness gate pass.
17. A non-maintainer or blind-run review confirms that developer knowledge was not required.
18. Documentation, CLI/help, package metadata, skills, product status, roadmap, current questions, and iQ state agree with the demonstrated product.
19. The full regression suite and focused acceptance/failure tests pass, with exact commands and results recorded.
20. The board reconvenes before changing release posture or expanding the roadmap.

- Just Works Continuity as the proposed next milestone.
- One immutable approved consumer channel and visible development mode.
- Pre-meeting readiness and exact provenance.

- Presenting Stakeholder Briefing as proven from unqualified history.
- Silently replacing the canonical roadmap.
