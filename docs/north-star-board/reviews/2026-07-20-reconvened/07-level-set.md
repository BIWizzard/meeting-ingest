# Operational Level Set

## Where The Product Truly Is Today

Meeting Ingest is a valuable, technically credible meeting-record engine in **internal/private alpha**. Sustained HTV use proves recurring value. It does not yet prove a coherent continuity product: the approved runtime is not product-visible, the authoritative history is mixed-generation and only partly current-valid, and the normal workflow still depends on maintainer expertise.

## What Has Been Completed

- Core deterministic ingestion for `.txt`, `.vtt`, and `.docx`.
- Structured artifact and signal generation.
- Provider validation, session handoff binding, hashing, IDs, ledger, archive, reconcile, duplicate handling, and date-control foundations.
- 231 passing repository tests at review time.
- Sustained power-user use in HTV.

## What Is Active

- Bounded Layer 5B cleanup/corruption-recovery carryover.
- Governance correction of dogfood evidence, runtime truth, and historical-continuity scope.
- No approved next-milestone implementation has begun.

## What Is Not Yet Proven

- Which approved executable and workflow will run in a consumer project.
- A canonical install, pin, update, and compatibility contract.
- A simple pre-meeting readiness verdict.
- One-request fresh ingest and recovery without expert intervention.
- The validity, adoption state, and derivation eligibility of the full HTV/Spelman history.
- Useful continuity or Stakeholder Briefing over qualified accumulated history.
- Non-maintainer usability.

## Proposed Next Milestone

**Just Works Continuity**

> Know which approved logic will run, process the next meeting through one normal agent request, and keep accumulated history usable and explainable without silent mutation.

The owner approved this milestone, Claude Code as the reference host, and the maintainer as the initial private-alpha audience on 2026-07-20.

## Deliverables

1. Approved immutable runtime, consumer pin/channel, build identity, and skill/workflow compatibility.
2. Product-visible `Ready`, `Ready with history warnings`, or `Blocked` preflight.
3. Exact build provenance in runs, handoffs, artifacts, ledgers, and derived outputs.
4. Honest pending/completion semantics and product-owned recovery.
5. Deterministic, privacy-safe, read-only HTV/Spelman corpus classification.
6. Separately approved adoption plan before any historical mutation.
7. One fresh real meeting through the reference host.
8. One qualified historical continuity proof with explicit coverage and exclusions.

## Measurable Exit Gate

The milestone exits when a consumer can verify the approved immutable build without source inspection; one normal host request completes a fresh meeting safely; failures and pending state are unmistakable and recoverable; the full authoritative corpus has a repeatable, non-mutating classification; any historical changes are separately approved and provenance-preserving; a qualified historical subset supports a useful continuity task; and documentation, roadmap, status, CLI, skills, tests, and release claims agree.

## What Must Not Expand Yet

Do not expand Guidance, sources, providers, hosts, global identity, deeper iQ integration, public launch, or Stakeholder Briefing claims. Do not mutate HTV/Spelman. Do not count or demonstrate the six copied local records. Do not allow an editable build to appear equivalent to an approved client build.

## Retirement Disposition

Exclude the six local records from all evidence immediately. Remove or archive their complete ignored runtime state only after uniqueness verification and explicit owner approval. Do not delete individual representations piecemeal.

## Decisions Awaiting Owner Approval

- History-warning readiness policy.
- Later corpus adoption posture.
- Minimum continuity/Briefing proof, artifact mutability, date policy, local-corpus disposition, and bounded Layer 5B closure.

## Reconvene

Reconvene after Approved Runtime and read-only Corpus Reckoning evidence exists and before any corpus mutation. Reconvene again before changing release posture or expanding beyond Just Works Continuity.
